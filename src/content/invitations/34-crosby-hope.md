*[TITLE CARD: "ROAD TO ARLINGTON"]*

*[ukulele sting]*

---

HOPE: Say Bing, where are we headed?

CROSBY: Arlington, Bob. Massachusetts. There's a barbecue.

HOPE: A barbecue! I love a barbecue. Who's cooking?

CROSBY: Jeannine.

HOPE: *[stops walking]*

HOPE: THE Jeannine?

CROSBY: The very same.

HOPE: *[to camera]* We're going.

---

CROSBY: We were always going, Bob.

HOPE: I knew that. I just wanted the audience to know that I knew that.

CROSBY: The audience knew you knew.

HOPE: Did they?

CROSBY: They're very sophisticated.

HOPE: *[looks at audience doubtfully]* If you say so.

---

CROSBY: Seventy Scituate Street. Rear garden. One o'clock. May the seventeenth.

HOPE: And we bring something.

CROSBY: We bring something.

HOPE: What are we bringing?

CROSBY: I thought you were bringing something.

HOPE: I thought YOU were bringing something.

CROSBY: Bob.

HOPE: Bing.

CROSBY: *[to camera]* He's not bringing anything.

HOPE: *[to camera]* He's not bringing anything either but he looks better doing it.

---

CROSBY: *[begins to croon, inexplicably]*
*There's a garden in Arlington...*
*Where the smoke rises up to the sky...*
*Where Jeannine tends the fire...*
*And you'd better not try...*
*To tell her the chicken needs turning...*
*Or ask her how long...*
*She's the queen of the grill, Bob...*
*And she's never been wrong...*

HOPE: *[wiping eye]* That was beautiful, Bing.

CROSBY: Thank you, Bob.

HOPE: I hated every second of it.

CROSBY: I know you did.

---

HOPE: What about Felice? Is Felice going to be there?

CROSBY: Felice has organized everything.

HOPE: *[genuine relief]* Oh thank God.

CROSBY: I know.

HOPE: No I mean it. Remember when Felice WASN'T handling things?

CROSBY: That was a different picture.

HOPE: Terrible picture.

CROSBY: Didn't do well at the box office.

HOPE: Nobody brought anything to share.

CROSBY: Coordination was not forthcoming.

HOPE: *[shudders]*

---

*[Dorothy Lamour appears, inexplicably, in the rear garden]*

LAMOUR: Boys! You made it!

HOPE: *[immediately straightening hair]* Dorothy! You look wonderful!

CROSBY: *[already at the food table]* She really does.

HOPE: Don't you have somewhere to be?

CROSBY: I'm at the food table, Bob. I'm exactly where I need to be.

HOPE: *[to camera]* He does this every picture. Every single picture.

LAMOUR: Jeannine's almost ready.

HOPE AND CROSBY: *[in unison]* JEANNINE.

*[they look at each other]*

*[they look at the camera]*

HOPE: How does she do it?

CROSBY: Every year, Bob. Every year.

---

*[Paramount logo appears in the sky for no reason]*

*[Hope looks up at it]*

HOPE: You know they're not even paying us for this one.

CROSBY: It's a barbecue, Bob. Nobody gets paid.

HOPE: *[to camera]*

Don't miss it though.

May seventeenth. One o'clock. Seventy Scituate Street, Arlington. The rear garden. Bring something.

CROSBY: *[already eating]*

Worth the trip.

HOPE: *[muttering, walking offscreen]*

He always gets to the food first. Forty years. Every picture. The food.

*[TITLE CARD: "THE END"]*
