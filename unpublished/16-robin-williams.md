*[bounces onto stage]*

HELLO ARLINGTON! NA-NU NA-NU!

*[pause]*

That's the only time I'm doing that. I promised myself. Moving on.

The BOSTON MAGIC LAB — say it with me — the BOSTON MAGIC LAB is having a BARBECUE and I need you to understand what that means because I have BEEN to this barbecue, I have SEEN what happens at this barbecue, I have WITNESSED Jeannine at that grill and let me tell you something —

*[stops]*

*[gets quiet]*

You know how there are people in this world — and there aren't many, there really aren't many — who are just. Exactly. Right. At the thing they do? Like the universe looked at the situation, looked at the person, said *yes. her. obviously. done.*

That's Jeannine.

*[back up to speed]*

SEVENTY SCITUATE STREET, ARLINGTON, MASSACHUSETTS, MAY SEVENTEENTH, ONE O'CLOCK, THE REAR GARDEN, BE THERE, BRING SOMETHING, FOOD OR DRINK, COORDINATION FORTHCOMING, FELICE IS HANDLING IT —

*[as several different people]*

Is Felice handling it? FELICE IS HANDLING IT. Are you sure? I'M SURE. How sure? VERY SURE. How very sure? SHE'S FELICE.

*[stops again]*

*[sits down on the edge of the stage]*

*[just talks]*

You know what I love about the Magic Lab? They did the thing. They actually did the thing. You know how many people talk about doing the thing? Everybody talks about doing the thing. The Magic Lab did the thing. And now the season's over and they want to sit in a garden with the people they did it with and eat food and just — be. Just be together for one afternoon in May before life starts up again.

That's not a barbecue.

That's everything.

That's the whole thing right there.

*[stands back up]*

*[the engine turns over]*

So you're going to go, RIGHT? You're going to be at seventy Scituate Street on May seventeenth at one o'clock in the rear garden with something to share, BECAUSE JEANNINE IS GOING TO BE AT THAT GRILL and the smoke is going to go up and Felice has ORGANIZED EVERYTHING and the Founders are going to be there FOUNDING and the Magic Lab is going to be there being MAGICAL and the May afternoon is going to do what May afternoons DO —

*[voice drops to almost nothing]*

and it's going to be really, really good.

*[pause]*

Go.

Go to the barbecue.

Bring something nice.

Tell Jeannine I said hey.

*[bows]*

*[starts to leave]*

*[turns back]*

Seriously though. Jeannine.

*[exits]*
